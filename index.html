Purchase Costs

These are the upfront fees that you'll need to budget for. We'll start you off by calculating the Stamp Duty as well as Transfer and Mortgage Registration fees.

Expense Amount ($)
Stamp Duty info_outline

Stamp Duty

Stamp Duty is a tax charged by the government on the purchase of a property. It's calculated based on the value of the property and varies by state or territory. You can expect this to be your biggest upfront purchase cost, and unfortunately cannot be included in your home loan amount.

Close
Title Transfer Fee info_outline

Title Transfer Fee

This is a state/territory government fee for transferring the property title from the seller to the buyer.

Close
Mortgage Registration Fee info_outline

Mortgage Registration Fee

This is a state/territory government fee for registering the lender's mortgage on the property's title record. For example, if you go for a home loan with a specific lender, this fee is for registering the lender's mortgage on the property.

Close
Conveyancing Fee info_outline

Conveyancing Fees

Conveyancing fees cover the legal work required to transfer property ownership including all the paperwork, searches, and ensuring everything is in order for the settlement.

An estimated range for conveyancing is $750 to $1,500 depending on complexity and location.

Close
Building Inspection info_outline

Building Inspection

A building inspection is a thorough examination of a property's condition, focusing on the structural and safety aspects. It helps identify any potential issues with the property.

An estimated range for building inspections is $250 to $600 depending on complexity and location. Many building inspectors will also offer pest inspection as part of their service for an additional fee. This is often cheaper than getting building and pest inspections performed by different businesses.

Close
Pest Inspection info_outline

Pest Inspection

A pest inspection checks for the presence of pests, such as termites or other wood-destroying insects, that can damage the property.

An estimated range for standalone pest inspections is $150 to $300 depending on complexity and location. Many building inspectors will also offer pest inspection as part of their service for an additional fee. This is often cheaper than getting building and pest inspections performed by different businesses.

Close
Building & contents insurance info_outline

Building & contents insurance

This insurance provides coverage for your home, its contents and any structures on your property. It protects against risks like fire, theft and other damages.

An estimated range for building and contents insurance is between $1,600 and $2,000. This can vary significantly based on location, property value and level of coverage.

Close
Council & water rates info_outline

Council & water rates

These are regular fees you'll need to pay for services like local government rates, water usage and waste services. This does not include your regular on-going bills like electricity and gas. When you buy a property, council and water rates are split between you and the seller based on how long each of you owns the home during the current billing period. Your conveyancer adjusts this at settlement. So if the seller has already paid for the quarter, you’ll reimburse them for your share. For example, if settlement is halfway through the quarter, you’ll pay them back for the remaining half.

An estimated range for council and water rates is between $1,500 and $2,500. This can vary significantly based on location, property value and level of coverage.

Close
Removalist info_outline

Removalist

This covers the cost of hiring a professional removalist or moving company to help you transport your belongings to your new home. Depending on your personal scenario, this may be a professional organisation, or a truck with a few friends.

Close
Refurnishing info_outline

Refurnishing

This is the budget for furnishing and decorating your new home. It includes buying new furniture and decor items.

Purchase Cost Total
Bank Fees

These are fees charged by the bank as part of the home loan application process. Some of these fees may be capitalised (added) to the loan amount depending on your lender. You may not know these details yet, but it's important to ask your lender for a breakdown of all fees involved.

Expense Amount ($)
Application Fee info_outline

Application Fee

The application fee is a one-time charge imposed by the lender when you apply for a home loan. It covers the cost of processing your loan application.

An estimated range is between $150 and $500. This can vary significantly based on lender (bank).

Close
Valuation Fee info_outline

Valuation Fee

The valuation fee is a charge for the assessment of the property's value, conducted by a qualified valuer. This assessment is essential for the lender to determine the property's worth. This fee will vary depending on the type of valuation required.

An estimated range is between $150 and $300. This can vary significantly based on lender (bank).

Close
Settlement Fee info_outline

Settlement Fee

The settlement fee covers the costs associated with the legal process of transferring property ownership. It includes the preparation of legal documents and settlement services.

An estimated range is between $150 and $300. This can vary significantly based on lender (bank).

Close
Package Fee info_outline

Package Fee

A optional package fee is an annual fee charged on a home loan that bundles it with other financial products, such as a credit card, savings account or insurance. It is designed to be a more cost-effective alternative to paying separate, individual fees on each product.

An estimated range is between $300 and $600. This can vary significantly based on lender (bank).

Total Transaction Amount info_outline

Total Transaction Amount

The total transaction amount is the full cost of buying the property. This is including the purchase price, upfront purchase costs (like stamp duty and legal fees) and any bank fees. It gives you a complete picture of how much you’ll need to complete the purchase, not just the price of the property itself.

Total transaction amount = Property purchase price + Purchase costs + Bank fees

Lenders Mortgage Insurance

Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI) is insurance your lender (the bank) takes out to protect themselves in case you can’t repay your loan. This is usually required if your deposit is less than 20% of the property value. The exact values will depend on your lender (bank).
