Purchase Costs

These are the upfront fees that you'll need to budget for. We'll start you off by calculating the Stamp Duty, Transfer and Mortgage Registration fees.

Expense Amount ($)
Stamp Duty info_outline

Stamp Duty

Stamp Duty is a tax charged by the government on the purchase of a property. It's calculated based on the value of the property and varies by state or territory. You can expect this to be your biggest upfront purchase cost, and unfortunately cannot be included in your home loan amount.

Close
Title Transfer Fee info_outline

Title Transfer Fee

This is a state/territory government fee for transferring the property title from the seller to the buyer.

Close
Mortgage Registration Fee info_outline

Mortgage Registration Fee

This is a state/territory government fee for registering the lender's mortgage on the property's title record. For example, if you go for a home loan with a specific lender, this fee is for registering the lender's mortgage on the property.

Home / Building / Contents insurance

This insurance provides coverage for your home, its contents, and any structures on your property. It protects against risks like fire, theft, and other damages.

Purchase Cost Total
Bank Fees

These are fees charged by the bank as part of the home loan application process. Some of these fees may be capitalised (added) to the loan amount depending on your lender.

Expense Amount ($)
Application Fee info_outline

Application Fee

The application fee is a one-time charge imposed by the lender when you apply for a home loan. It covers the cost of processing your loan application.

Close
Valuation Fee info_outline

Valuation Fee

The valuation fee is a charge for the assessment of the property's value, conducted by a qualified valuer. This assessment is essential for the lender to determine the property's worth. This fee will vary depending on the type of valuation required.

Close
Settlement Fee info_outline

Settlement Fee

The settlement fee covers the costs associated with the legal process of transferring property ownership. It includes the preparation of legal documents and settlement services.

Close
Package Fee info_outline

Package Fee

The package fee is associated with certain loan packages offered by lenders. These packages may include additional features such as offset accounts or discounted interest rates.

Lenders Mortgage Insurance

Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI) is insurance that a lender takes out to insure itself against the risk of not recovering the outstanding loan balance if you, the borrower, are unable to meet your loan payments and the property is sold for less than the outstanding loan balance.
